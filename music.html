Country Music.Yeeeehaw

America might be burning, but its music is thriving

Within the last year we've seen a rise in the popularity of country music; And I, admittingly, have been swept up in the storm of the sweet ring of harmonicas, warms strings of acoustic guitars, and lyrics that romanticize staring at the vast horizons atop the desert landscape. While I am forever a fan of rap, hip-hop, and rnb (music that falls under the problematic label of "urban"), my interest in country comes at time where I have just finished undergrad and I am beginning to think of the lifestyle I want to work towards for my future self; slow, family-oriented, and appreciative of the little things. These desires did not happen in a vaccuum, with an oversaturated news and social media feed, the yearning for a slow life is a phenomenon felt by many trying to escape the noise and chatter that-- if not faced in our physical environment-- is forced down our throats by way of blue-lit screen. Maybe I can't turn off my phone, but I can tune into Zach Bryan or Tyler Childers and imagine that I am waiting for tumble weeds to pass on by.
Songs I've been enjoying: Your patience, it ran so high by harf., American Nights by Zach Bryan, and Shake the Frost (live) by Tyler Childers.
